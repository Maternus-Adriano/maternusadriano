The Problem

Where East African health systems
consistently break down

PPP Frameworks
Agreements that look good on paper, fail in practice

Public–private partnerships in healthcare are only as strong as the operational infrastructure beneath them. Misaligned KPIs, unclear accountability, and poor coordination between institutional partners erode outcomes even with adequate funding.

Service Delivery
Clinical capacity without the operational backbone

Hospitals and health facilities that invest in clinical expertise but neglect scheduling, claims, procurement, and patient flow consistently underperform — frustrating patients, staff, and funders alike.

Island & Regional Systems
Referral and coordination gaps that cost lives

In archipelago systems like Zanzibar, inter-island referral failures are not clinical problems — they are logistical and institutional ones. The same applies across Tanzania's Southern Highlands: distance amplifies every coordination failure.

Institutional Alignment
Strategies that never reach the ground

Ministries, donors, and hospital boards frequently invest in strategy without the executive coordination infrastructure to implement it. The gap between policy intent and frontline delivery is where most interventions lose their impact.

Selected Work

Three situations.
What actually happened.

"What do you actually do?" Here are three honest accounts — the situation, the challenge, and the outcome.

Zanzibar · 2025 · Digital Health
Taking Pemba's Hospitals from Paper Records into the Digital Age

When NSK Hospitals Ltd took on management of hospitals in Pemba under the PPP with the Ministry of Health, the facilities were still running on paper-based systems — a significant gap compared to Unguja. Part of the work was pushing that forward: integrating the Hospital Information System and leading the telemedicine pilot connecting facilities across the islands. It wasn't just a tech rollout; it meant navigating institutional sign-offs across both government and private systems while keeping daily operations running.

→ Pemba facilities moved from paper to HIS; telemedicine pilot operational — a meaningful step forward for an island that had been left behind in digital health.
Southern Highlands · 2021–2024 · Regional Operations
Managing and Growing a Four-Region Dialysis Network

Africa Healthcare Network gave me responsibility for the Southern Highlands Zone — Mbeya, Ruvuma, Iringa, and Dodoma — at a point when it needed both structure and growth. I focused on the things that actually determine whether a network performs: getting the hospital partnerships right, fixing the billing and claims processes that were leaking revenue, tightening patient scheduling, and making sure the government relationships in each region were solid. Four regions at once, with very different local dynamics.

→ Regional financial performance improved meaningfully; hospital partnerships grew. Recognised with the AHN All Values Award — the organisation's highest internal recognition.
Tanzania · 2018 · National Policy
Contributing to Tanzania's National Nutrition Policy

As Project Administrator at ONA Foundation, I was part of the team that contributed to Tanzania's National School Feeding Guideline and the NMNAP — working alongside government bodies and international organisations. I also set up nutrition clubs across schools, reaching thousands of students, and helped orchestrate the fundraising that made the programmes viable.

→ Institutional knowledge embedded in national policy; school nutrition programmes established across Tanzania reaching thousands of students directly.
How I Think

A practitioner's
point of view

"Most healthcare failures in East Africa are not caused by a lack of resources. They are caused by a lack of institutional alignment — between strategy and operations, between government intent and frontline execution, between what donors fund and what systems actually need."

Systems Strengthening

"The PPP model is powerful. But a PPP that runs on goodwill and handshakes rather than clear accountability structures and operational discipline will underperform every time — regardless of how well-intentioned both parties are."

Public–Private Partnerships

"Fixing inter-island or inter-regional referral systems is not a clinical challenge. It requires integrated logistical planning, inter-institutional coordination, and political will — in that order."

Island & Regional Health Systems

"The Chief of Staff role in healthcare is underestimated. Executive coordination is a systems function — not an administrative one. Done well, it is the connective tissue between a director's vision and the hospital's daily reality."
